From Student to Institution Builder

"What capability should Botswana possess that it currently does not?"

Supply Chain Circle was founded by Katlo Galenamong, a Supply Chain Management student at the University of Botswana. SCC did not begin as an academic assignment — it began from a broader realization.

Throughout his studies, Katlo observed that procurement and supply chain management are often taught through theoretical frameworks while many practical challenges faced by Botswana remain insufficiently researched or digitally supported.

Rather than viewing technology simply as a means of automation, Katlo views software as a mechanism for creating institutional knowledge.

Our Philosophy

Five Guiding Principles

Everything developed under SCC follows these core principles.

01

Knowledge Before Software

Software should never exist simply because technology allows it. Every digital platform must solve an identifiable knowledge problem.

02

Methodology Before Implementation

A robust methodology has greater long-term value than software code. Technology changes. Methodologies endure.

03

Evidence Before Opinion

Institutional credibility is built through measurable evidence rather than personal views. Support recommendations with research, data, and experimentation.

04

Systems Thinking

Procurement is a complex adaptive system consisting of governments, suppliers, markets, regulations, and people. Consider interactions, not isolated components.

05

Botswana First

Although internationally informed, SCC methodologies reflect Botswana's institutional environment, legislation, and development priorities before expanding regionally.
